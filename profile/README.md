## The Problem

Nigerian real estate is broken at the trust layer.

Listings are duplicated, inflated, or outright fake. Agents operate with zero accountability — no verification, no history, no consequences. Buyers and renters send money to strangers based on WhatsApp screenshots and verbal promises. Landlords deal with time-wasters and fraudulent tenants. The entire market runs on social pressure and luck.

This isn't a niche complaint. It's the default experience for anyone trying to rent or buy property in Nigeria today.

The platforms that exist either ignore the problem entirely or treat it as someone else's job. They are aggregators, not infrastructure. They list properties without verifying who listed them, why the price is what it is, or whether the person on the other end of the inquiry is legitimate.

Xavorian is built to fix this — not by adding a disclaimer, but by making trust a core product feature.

## Core Features

### Agent Verification & KYC
Every agent on Xavorian goes through identity verification before they can publish listings or interact with seekers. Verified agents receive a trust badge that is displayed on their profile and every listing they post. This is not optional and it is not cosmetic — it is the baseline for operating on the platform.

Unverified listings exist but are clearly distinguished. The goal is to make verification the path of least resistance, and to make the trust badge something agents want because seekers actively filter for it.

### Scam Detection
We run integrity checks on listings at the point of submission. Duplicate detection, price anomaly flagging, suspicious contact pattern analysis — the goal is to catch fraudulent listings before they go live, not after someone has already lost money.

This system is trained on the specific fraud patterns common to the Nigerian property market: ghost listings, bait-and-switch properties, fake agent profiles, inflated prices with manufactured urgency.

### Structured Property Data
Most Nigerian real estate platforms suffer from inconsistent, low-quality listing data — vague descriptions, missing amenities, unverified addresses. Xavorian enforces structured data input at the listing level so that every property on the platform is comparable, searchable, and honest.

### Location Intelligence
We have built programmatic location coverage across Benin City's major neighborhoods spanning Oredo, Egor, and Ikpoba-Okha LGAs. Each area has associated data on typical pricing, property types, and neighborhood context — so seekers can make informed decisions without relying entirely on what an agent tells them.

### Payments & Payouts
Transactions on Xavorian are handled through Paystack, with automated agent payouts built in. This removes the grey area around payment — both parties know what was paid, when, and to whom.

## Why Benin City First

Most Nigerian startups default to Lagos. We chose Benin City deliberately.

Benin City is a high-density urban market with real, underserved demand for trustworthy property discovery. It is large enough to validate the model but tight enough to build real density fast. The social networks are tighter, word-of-mouth travels faster, and the competitive landscape from tech-native platforms is thinner.

We are not building a thin national layer. We are building deep local infrastructure — one city, done properly — and expanding from a position of proven traction rather than assumed demand.

## The Bigger Picture

Trust infrastructure in Nigerian real estate is the unsexy, hard work that nobody wants to do because it does not feel like a product feature. It feels like compliance, or operations, or legal overhead.

We think that is exactly why it is defensible.

Marketplaces that solve trust at the infrastructure level — not as a bolt-on, but as the core mechanic — are significantly harder to replicate than those that compete on design or inventory. Every verified agent, every flagged scam, every completed transaction with a clean audit trail makes the platform more valuable and harder to displace.

We are building the trust layer for Nigerian real estate. The marketplace is the front door.
